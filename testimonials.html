I wanted to share my experience at Seliana, as it was refreshing and noteworthy to encounter horse people of such caliber. I have a collection of complicated mares that require precise, careful handling, one of which is the Icelandic Irpadis fra Schmaltztopf. Irpadis is the best of the Schmaltztopf program's domestic production in terms of conformation and courage, but has unfortunately been "cowboyed" in her lifetime, leaving her with a nearly severed tongue and an absolute mistrust of strangers.
I brought her to Cathleen and Kristian's farm to be bred to their lovely stallion Svalur. Initially, I had planned to stay for the week and supervise my mare to ensure her confidence and safety in the new environment. However, I was able to enjoy watching the unfolding of a minor miracle, as my mare and I observed the horses at Seliana's complete confidence in their handling and training. Irpadis voted in favor of Cathleen and Kristian as well, permitting herself to be handled with aplomb.
I felt confident letting my sensitive, damaged mare stay for a month under their care, given the trust exhibited by their horses and my mare's clear demonstration of her willingness to be handled.
My life's calling has become horses, and I work at an 80 horse breeding program, focusing on modifying the behavior of opinionated broodmares, resulting in the production of happy, well adjusted foals. I have seen a lot of management styles for horses, both good and bad. I would feel comfortable sending any of my special needs mares to stay with Cathleen and Kristian at a moment's notice, as I know they will be cared for with the kindness and attention to detail given to a favorite horse.

Maggie B.

West Virginia, USA
